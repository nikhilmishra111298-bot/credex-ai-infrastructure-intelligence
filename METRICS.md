# Product Metrics

## Overview

This document defines the primary metrics for evaluating the Credex AI Spend Audit platform.

The goal is to measure:
- engagement
- product usefulness
- conversion quality
- growth potential

---

# Core Product Metrics

## Completed Audits

Definition:
Number of users who successfully run an audit.

Why it matters:
- primary activation event
- indicates product engagement

---

## Lead Capture Rate

Definition:
Percentage of completed audits that submit contact information.

Formula:

lead captures / completed audits

Why it matters:
- measures funnel quality
- determines business value

---

## Share Link Usage

Definition:
Number of copied/shared audit links.

Why it matters:
- measures virality
- indicates perceived usefulness

---

## PDF Export Rate

Definition:
Percentage of users exporting reports.

Why it matters:
- signals high-intent users
- indicates professional/internal usage

---

# Financial Metrics

## Average Estimated Savings

Definition:
Average monthly savings identified across audits.

Why it matters:
- validates optimization value
- supports marketing claims

---

## Total Potential Savings Identified

Definition:
Aggregate savings identified across all users.

Why it matters:
- demonstrates platform impact
- useful for marketing/social proof

---

## Savings Percentage

Definition:
Average percent reduction opportunity across audits.

Formula:

monthly savings / current spend

Why it matters:
- measures optimization severity

---

# Product Quality Metrics

## Health Score Distribution

Tracks:
- low-risk users
- medium-risk users
- high-risk users

Why it matters:
- measures customer segmentation
- identifies enterprise opportunities

---

## Error Rate

Tracks:
- failed submissions
- rendering issues
- API failures

Why it matters:
- platform reliability

---

## Build Success Rate

Measured through:
- GitHub Actions CI
- lint/test success

Why it matters:
- deployment stability
- developer reliability

---

# Growth Metrics

## Repeat Usage

Definition:
Users returning to run multiple audits.

Why it matters:
- long-term usefulness
- retention signal

---

## Team Expansion Potential

Tracks:
- team size
- seat growth
- increasing spend

Why it matters:
- identifies expansion revenue opportunities
