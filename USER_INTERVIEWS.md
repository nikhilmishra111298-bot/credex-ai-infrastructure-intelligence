# User Interview Notes

## Goal

The purpose of these interviews is to understand:
- AI tooling usage
- spending behavior
- procurement pain points
- optimization opportunities

The interviews are exploratory and intended to validate product-market fit.

---

# Interview Group 1 — Startup Founder

## Team Size

5 people

---

## AI Stack

- ChatGPT Plus
- Cursor
- Claude

---

## Pain Points

- unclear total monthly spend
- duplicated subscriptions
- difficult to compare value across tools

---

## Observations

The founder was:
- highly cost-sensitive
- interested in optimization
- unaware of overlapping usage

The idea of a centralized audit dashboard was positively received.

---

# Interview Group 2 — Engineering Team Lead

## Team Size

15 developers

---

## AI Stack

- GitHub Copilot
- ChatGPT Team
- Cursor

---

## Pain Points

- unmanaged seat growth
- inconsistent tooling adoption
- unclear ROI on AI subscriptions

---

## Observations

The team lead wanted:
- visibility into spend
- organization-wide reporting
- easier procurement decisions

The PDF export and dashboard analytics were viewed as valuable.

---

# Interview Group 3 — Operations / Finance

## Team Size

30 employees

---

## AI Stack

- ChatGPT Enterprise
- Claude Team
- Gemini

---

## Pain Points

- AI invoices spread across departments
- budgeting unpredictability
- limited centralized governance

---

## Observations

Operations users cared more about:
- total spend visibility
- governance
- forecasting

Less interest in developer-specific recommendations.

---

# Key Themes Across Interviews

## 1. Visibility Problems

Most teams:
- do not know total AI spend
- lack centralized reporting

---

## 2. Duplicated Tooling

Common overlaps:
- ChatGPT + Claude
- Cursor + Copilot
- multiple unused seats

---

## 3. Procurement Friction

Teams struggle with:
- comparing plans
- deciding which tools to standardize on
- predicting future costs

---

## 4. Interest in Optimization

All interview groups showed interest in:
- reducing unnecessary spend
- benchmarking usage
- centralized AI management

---

# Product Insights

Most valuable features:
- health score
- savings calculations
- recommendations
- PDF export
- executive dashboard feel

---

# Risks Identified

Potential concerns:
- recommendation accuracy
- trust in savings estimates
- AI hallucinations in financial reporting

This reinforced the decision to:
- use deterministic calculations
- keep AI limited to summaries only
